About Me

I am Milad Yousefi, a passionate individual deeply immersed in the realms of mathematics, artificial intelligence, and neuroscience. My academic journey began at Tabriz University, Iran, where I pursued a Bachelor of Science in Applied Mathematics. Throughout my studies, I delved into the fascinating world of fuzzy modeling techniques, particularly focusing on their application in LIF neuron models. My research endeavors have been diverse, ranging from predicting brain strokes and exploring treatments for tinnitus to segmenting lung cancer and developing innovative image compression methods using singular value decomposition. As a teaching assistant, I've had the privilege of sharing my knowledge with bachelor students, guiding them through courses in differential equations, combinatorics, and linear optimization. Beyond academia, I've honed my skills in various programming languages and completed online courses in advanced bioinformatics, computational neuroscience, and deep learning. With an unwavering commitment to innovation and a thirst for advancing scientific knowledge, I am excited about the opportunities to contribute meaningfully to the fields of mathematics, AI, and neuroscience.
